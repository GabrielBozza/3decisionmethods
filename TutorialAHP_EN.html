● The AHP method is indicated for:
○ Decision Methods that take into consideration subjective aspects, which have more complex measurements, as they are of a personal nature and difficult to externalize. The benefit is that, since the judgment values of the parity comparisons are based on experience, intuition and also on physical data, AHP can deal with qualitative and quantitative aspects of a decision problem.
○ When teams are involved in complex, high-stakes problems that need human perception and whose resolution will have long-term repercussions.

Note: There is a limit of 15 alternatives and 15 criteria in order to make it possible to calculate the consistency of the method. It’s important to remember that the tolerance of inconsistencies isn’t a limitation, but a portrait of the reality.

● More about the AHP Method:
○ The Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP) is a method to auxiliate people in complex decision Methods, helping to determine which one is the correct decision and also how to justify this choice. It is based on mathematics and psychology and was developed in the 1970s by Prof. Thomas Saaty, at Wharton's choice at the University of Pennsylvania.
○ In this method, the decision makers evaluate the elements, comparing them in pairs, using the Saaty Scale (specifications in the further reading material), being able to use hard data or their judgments.
