● The TOPSIS method is indicated for:
○ Cases with many alternatives and criteria, since there is no limitation on the number of these fields.
○ Cases whose criteria are only quantitative and monotonically increasing or decreasing.
Note: it is inappropriate for decision Methods under uncertainty.

● More about the TOPSIS Method:
○ The TOPSIS algorithm (Technique for Order Preference by Similarity to Ideal Solution) was developed by Hwang & Yoon (1981) and is a technique for evaluating the performance of alternatives through its similarity with an ideal solution. According to this technique, the best alternative is the one closest to the ideal solution and the furthest from the non-ideal solution.
